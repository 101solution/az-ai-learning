# Emergency Management and Response

## Purpose
This plan describes how the operation prepares for and responds to emergencies, consistent
with the WA **Code of Practice — Emergency management for Western Australian mines**. The aim
is to protect life, then the environment, then property and production, in that order.

## Emergency types covered
Fire (mobile plant, infrastructure, bushfire), uncontrolled fall of ground, inrush of water
or material, hazardous material or reagent release, vehicle collision, medical emergency,
and missing person.

## Alarm and communication
- The **emergency radio channel is Channel 1**. Broadcast your name, location, and the nature
  of the emergency, and follow the directions of the Emergency Coordinator.
- The site evacuation alarm is a continuous siren. On hearing it, make your equipment safe,
  shut down where required, and proceed to your nearest **muster point**.
- Do not use the emergency channel for routine traffic during an incident.

## Muster and head count
Each work area has a designated muster point. Supervisors conduct a **head count** against the
crew list and report numbers to the Emergency Coordinator. Do not leave the muster point or
return to the incident area until the all-clear is given.

## Emergency Response Team (ERT)
The operation maintains a trained **Emergency Response Team** with members on every crew. The
ERT covers fire-fighting, vehicle extrication, confined-space and vertical rescue, hazardous
materials response, and advanced first aid. ERT members are released from normal duties when
activated.

## Self-escape and refuge
Workers in underground or enclosed areas must carry or have ready access to a **self-rescuer**
and know the route to the nearest refuge chamber. Refuge chambers provide breathable air,
communications, and water for the rated occupancy and duration.

## Medical response
Contact the site medic via the radio or the emergency number posted in every crib hut. For a
serious casualty, the Emergency Coordinator arranges retrieval and, if required, aeromedical
evacuation (RFDS) to the nearest hospital.

## Training and exercises
All workers complete emergency response awareness at induction. The site runs **emergency
exercises** at least quarterly, including at least one full evacuation drill per year, and
reviews the plan after every exercise or actual activation.
