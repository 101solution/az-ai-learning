# Haul Truck Maintenance Schedule

## Overview
This document covers preventive maintenance for the ultra-class haul truck fleet used in the
iron ore pit. Adhering to the schedule maximises availability and prevents unplanned downtime.
All maintenance is recorded in the CMMS against the asset number.

## Pre-start inspection (every shift)
Operators complete a pre-start checklist before operating any haul truck:

- Tyre condition and inflation; check for cuts, separations, and rim damage.
- Brake function test and park-brake hold test on a graded surface.
- Steering, horn, lights, reversing alarm, and rotating beacon.
- Fluid levels: engine oil, coolant, hydraulic oil, fuel.
- Fire suppression system indicator shows charged and armed.
- Cab: seatbelt, mirrors, two-way radio, operator screen, and collision-avoidance display.

Any defect that affects safe operation is a "red" item: tag out the truck and report it.

## Scheduled servicing intervals
- **250 hours**: engine oil and filter change, visual hydraulic inspection, grease.
- **500 hours**: transmission and differential oil sample, brake-cooling check.
- **1,000 hours**: major service — replace filters, inspect suspension struts, NDT inspection
  of the chassis and tray welds.
- **5,000 hours**: component condition assessment; plan for wheel-motor change-out.

## Tyre management
Tyres are the highest single consumable cost. Maintain cold inflation pressure to the
manufacturer specification for the loaded gross vehicle mass. Record **TKPH** (tonne-kilometre
per hour) and rotate tyres per the wear plan. Do not exceed the rated speed for the tyre, and
manage haul-road condition to reduce cuts and heat build-up.

## Fire suppression
The engine-bay fire suppression system is inspected at every 250-hour service and
function-tested every 1,000 hours. A discharged or faulted system is an immediate
out-of-service condition.

## Isolation for maintenance
Before any maintenance, the truck is isolated and locked out per the isolation procedure:
engine off, park brake applied, wheels chocked, articulation/tray secured, and stored energy
(hydraulic and electrical) dissipated and verified at zero.
