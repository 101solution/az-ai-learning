# Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines the minimum personal protective equipment for all operational areas.
PPE is the last line of defence in the hierarchy of controls and must be worn correctly at
all times in designated areas, in addition to higher-order controls.

## Mandatory site-wide PPE
All personnel and visitors entering operational areas must wear, as a minimum:

- Hard hat compliant with **AS/NZS 1801**, worn with the brim facing forward.
- Safety glasses with side shields (**AS/NZS 1337.1**), clear or tinted for conditions.
- High-visibility shirt or vest rated day/night (**AS/NZS 4602.1**).
- Steel-capped safety boots with ankle support (**AS/NZS 2210.3**).
- Hearing protection (Class 5) in posted high-noise zones (**AS/NZS 1270**).

## Haul road and heavy-vehicle areas
Any person on foot near the haul road or in active heavy-vehicle areas must wear the full
site-wide PPE **plus** the following:

- Full-length high-visibility outerwear with retroreflective tape on arms and legs.
- A personal duress beacon or two-way radio kept on the designated channel.
- Cut-resistant gloves when handling tyres, chains, or steel rope.

Pedestrians must never enter the haul road without positive radio communication with the
relevant operators and a confirmed exclusion zone. Light vehicles on the haul road must run
headlights, a rotating beacon, and a buggy whip flag at all times.

## Respiratory protection
A **P2** half-face respirator is the minimum for dry cutting, grinding, or any task that
generates dust above the posted exposure limit, including work with potential **respirable
crystalline silica**. Respirators must be fit-tested annually and users must be clean-shaven
at the seal. Higher-rated respiratory protection applies in the processing plant where
reagents or fumes are present — refer to the task risk assessment and SDS.

## Inspection and replacement
Inspect PPE before each shift. Remove damaged PPE from service immediately and obtain a
replacement from the store. Hard hats are replaced every three years, or after any
significant impact, whichever comes first.
