# Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines the minimum personal protective equipment required across all
operational areas of the site. PPE is the last line of defence in the hierarchy of
controls and must be worn correctly at all times in designated areas.

## Mandatory site-wide PPE
All personnel and visitors entering operational areas must wear, as a minimum:

- Hard hat (compliant with AS/NZS 1801), worn with the brim facing forward.
- Safety glasses with side shields, clear or tinted as appropriate for conditions.
- High-visibility shirt or vest rated day/night (AS/NZS 4602.1).
- Steel-capped safety boots (AS/NZS 2210.3) with ankle support.
- Hearing protection (Class 5) in posted high-noise zones.

## Haul road and heavy-vehicle areas
Any person on foot near the haul road or in active heavy-vehicle areas must wear the
full site-wide PPE **plus** the following:

- Full-length high-visibility outerwear with reflective tape on arms and legs.
- A personal duress beacon or radio kept on the designated channel.
- Cut-resistant gloves when handling tyres, chains, or steel rope.

Pedestrians must never enter the haul road without positive radio communication with
the relevant operators and a confirmed exclusion zone. Light vehicles on the haul road
must run headlights, a rotating beacon, and a buggy whip flag at all times.

## Respiratory protection
A P2 half-face respirator is required during dry cutting, grinding, or any task that
generates dust above the posted exposure limit. Respirators must be fit-tested annually.

## Inspection and replacement
Inspect PPE before each shift. Remove damaged PPE from service immediately and obtain
a replacement from the store. Hard hats are replaced every three years or after any
significant impact, whichever comes first.
