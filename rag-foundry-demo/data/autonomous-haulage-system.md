# Autonomous Haulage System (AHS) Operations

## Scope
This procedure covers safe operation around the **autonomous haulage system (AHS)** in the
iron ore pit, modelled on the WA **Code of Practice — Safe mobile autonomous mining**.
Autonomous haul trucks operate without an in-cab operator within a defined **Autonomous
Operating Zone (AOZ)**.

## The Autonomous Operating Zone (AOZ)
The AOZ is a physically and procedurally controlled area. Entry is strictly managed:
- The AOZ boundary is defined, signed, and bunded, with controlled access points.
- No person or manned vehicle enters the AOZ without **authorisation and a defined
  interaction control** in place.
- Entry is logged and the central controller is notified before and after entry.

## Interaction controls
Light vehicles and manned equipment that must enter the AOZ are fitted with **AHS-compatible
interaction units** that make them visible to autonomous trucks and enforce separation. The
key controls are:
- **Exclusion zones**: an autonomous truck will not enter an area locked out by an authorised
  person; conversely, manned vehicles are assigned permission slots.
- **Speed and separation**: autonomous trucks maintain mandated separation and slow or stop
  when an object or vehicle breaches their safety envelope.
- **Positive communication** with the AHS central controller before any movement that affects
  the AOZ.

## Stopping an autonomous truck
Any authorised person can bring autonomous trucks to a controlled stop using the **manual
intervention / e-stop** controls — in-vehicle, at the controller, or via the area exclusion
lockout. Always assume a truck cannot see you until separation is confirmed by the system.

## Recovery and maintenance
Approaching a stopped autonomous truck for recovery, refuelling, or maintenance requires the
truck to be placed in a **safe, isolated state** with the area exclusion applied and confirmed
by the central controller before anyone enters its working radius.

## Change management
Changes to the AOZ — new roads, dump locations, or interacting equipment — go through the AHS
management of change process and are validated before autonomous operation resumes.
