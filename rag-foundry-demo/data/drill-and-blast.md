# Drill and Blast Procedure

## Scope
This procedure governs the safe storage, handling, and use of explosives and the conduct of
blasting in the open pit. Explosives work is a **principal hazard** and is managed under the
explosives management plan, consistent with WA dangerous goods and mining safety requirements.

## Competency and authority
Only licensed **shotfirers** may charge, prime, and fire blasts. All explosives handling is
supervised by an authorised person, and personnel hold the relevant explosives competencies.

## Storage and transport
Explosives and detonators are stored separately in licensed magazines, secured and inventoried.
The magazine register is reconciled each shift; any discrepancy is reported immediately.
Bulk emulsion is delivered and mixed by a mobile manufacturing unit (MMU) at the blast pattern.

## Charging the pattern
- The blast area is cordoned and only essential, authorised personnel are permitted.
- Detonators are kept separate from boosters until priming.
- Mobile phones and unauthorised radio transmitters are prohibited near the charged pattern.
- Charged holes are stemmed and the pattern tied in per the blast design.

## Blast exclusion zone and firing
Before firing, a **blast exclusion zone** is established and confirmed clear by sentries and a
documented clearance sweep. Personnel and equipment are withdrawn to the calculated safe
distance for flyrock, fume, and ground vibration. The firing sequence is:
1. Final clearance and head count — all people and plant outside the exclusion zone.
2. Sentries posted on every access road with positive radio confirmation.
3. Audible warning signals sounded (warning, fire, and all-clear signals are distinct).
4. Fire on the shotfirer's authority only.

## Misfires
A suspected **misfire** is a serious hazard. Do not approach the pattern. Apply the misfire
waiting period, keep the exclusion zone in place, notify the shotfirer and supervisor, and
follow the documented misfire procedure. Only the shotfirer manages misfire make-safe.

## Post-blast
Re-entry occurs only after the **all-clear**, once fume has cleared and the area is inspected
for unfired explosives, unstable ground, and toxic post-blast gases.
