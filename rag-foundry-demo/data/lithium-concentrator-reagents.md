# Lithium Concentrator — Reagent and Process Safety

## Scope
This procedure covers safe handling of reagents and process hazards in the **spodumene
(lithium) concentrator**, where ore is crushed, ground, and upgraded by **dense media
separation (DMS) and froth flotation** to produce spodumene concentrate. Reagents are
**dangerous goods** and are managed under the operation's hazardous chemicals plan.

## Reagents on site
Typical flotation and processing reagents include collectors (fatty acids/amines), frothers,
pH modifiers such as **sodium hydroxide (caustic) and sulfuric acid**, depressants, and
flocculants. Each reagent has a current **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** available at the point of
use and in the chemical register.

## Before handling any reagent
- Read the **SDS** and the task risk assessment; know the hazards, first aid, and spill response.
- Wear the PPE specified by the SDS — typically chemical-splash goggles, face shield, chemical
  gloves and apron, and the correct respiratory protection for the reagent.
- Confirm eyewash and safety showers nearby are operational and unobstructed.

## Storage and segregation
Reagents are stored in **bunded** areas sized to contain the largest vessel plus rainfall, with
**incompatible chemicals segregated** — acids away from caustics, oxidisers away from fuels and
organics. Dangerous goods are placarded and manifested per the dangerous goods code.

## Acid and caustic handling
Acid and caustic cause severe burns. Always **add acid to water**, never water to acid. Use
dedicated, labelled transfer equipment. Decant and dose only through engineered systems where
provided. Treat every line as live until isolated, drained, and verified.

## Spills and releases
Contain the spill if safe to do so using the spill kit for that chemical class, prevent entry
to drains and the environment, and notify the supervisor and environment team. A reagent
release is a reportable environmental incident.

## Confined spaces and isolation
Entry to tanks, cells, mills, and thickeners requires a **confined space permit** and full
energy isolation (lockout) of mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, and process-fluid energy,
with atmospheric testing before and during entry.
