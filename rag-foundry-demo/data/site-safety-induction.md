# Site Safety Induction

## Before you arrive
No one may enter site without a current induction. Complete the online induction module
and pass the assessment, then attend the on-site verification of competency on your first
day. Bring photo ID, your tickets (e.g. high-risk work licences), and evidence of a
fitness-for-work medical where required for your role.

## Speed limits and traffic
- Light vehicles: 40 km/h on sealed roads, 20 km/h in the workshop and pit areas.
- Always give way to heavy vehicles; they have limited visibility and cannot stop quickly.
- Maintain positive communication on the pit radio channel before entering the pit.
- Park in designated areas only, wheels chocked, facing the direction of escape.

## The hierarchy of controls
We control risk in this order of preference: eliminate, substitute, isolate (engineering),
administrative controls, and finally PPE. PPE alone is never an acceptable primary control
for a significant hazard.

## Permits and high-risk work
A permit to work is required for: working at heights, confined space entry, hot work,
isolation of energy, excavation, and crane lifts. Permits are issued by an authorised
permit officer and must be closed out at the end of the task.

## Emergency response
- Emergency channel on the radio is Channel 1. State your name, location, and the nature
  of the emergency.
- Know your muster point. On the evacuation alarm, make equipment safe and proceed to the
  nearest muster point for a head count.
- First aid: contact the medic via the radio or the emergency number posted in every crib hut.

## Your right and duty to stop work
Every worker has the authority to stop any task they believe is unsafe, without fear of
reprisal. Stop the job, make it safe, and escalate to your supervisor. When in doubt, stop.
