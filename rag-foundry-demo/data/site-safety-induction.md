# Site Safety Induction

## Legislative framework
Work at this operation is governed by the **Work Health and Safety Act 2020 (WA)** and the
**Work Health and Safety (Mines) Regulations 2022 (WA)**. Every worker, contractor, and
visitor has duties under the Act, and the mine operator must manage risk so far as is
reasonably practicable (SFAIRP).

## Before you arrive
No person may enter the operation without a current induction. Complete the online general
induction and pass the assessment, then attend the on-site verification of competency (VOC)
on your first day. Bring photo ID, your high-risk work licences and tickets, and evidence of
a fitness-for-work medical assessment where required for your role.

## The hierarchy of controls
Risk is controlled in this order of preference, consistent with the WHS (Mines) Regulations:
**elimination, substitution, isolation, engineering controls, administrative controls, and
PPE.** PPE alone is never an acceptable primary control for a significant or principal hazard.

## Principal hazards and management plans
The operation maintains **principal hazard management plans (PHMPs)** for the hazards that
could cause multiple fatalities, including: ground or strata failure, mobile plant and
vehicle interactions, fire and explosion, inrush of water or material, and air quality and
dust. Work that touches a principal hazard must follow the relevant PHMP and any associated
safe work procedure.

## Speed limits and traffic
- Light vehicles: 40 km/h on sealed roads, 20 km/h in the workshop, ROM pad, and pit.
- Always give way to heavy vehicles — they have large blind spots and cannot stop quickly.
- Establish **positive communication** on the pit radio channel before entering the pit.
- Park in designated areas only, wheels chocked, parked in the direction of escape.

## Mobile phones and driver distraction
Using a **hand-held mobile phone while operating any vehicle or mobile plant is prohibited**,
including while driving on the haul road. Driver distraction is a recognised cause of serious
vehicle incidents. If you need to use a phone, first **stop in a designated safe parking area**
with the vehicle secured (park brake on, out of traffic). While driving, communication is via
the **fixed, hands-free two-way radio** on the designated channel only — not a personal phone.
Phones and unauthorised transmitters are also prohibited near charged blast patterns.

## Permits and high-risk work
A permit to work is required for: working at heights, confined space entry, hot work,
isolation of hazardous energy, excavation and penetration, and crane and lifting operations.
Permits are issued by an authorised permit officer and must be closed out at the end of the task.

## Emergency response basics
The emergency channel on the radio is **Channel 1**. State your name, your location, and the
nature of the emergency. Know your muster point; on the evacuation alarm, make equipment safe
and proceed to the nearest muster point for a head count.

## Your right and duty to stop work
Every worker has the authority to **stop any task they believe is unsafe**, without fear of
reprisal. Stop the job, make the area safe, and escalate to your supervisor. When in doubt, stop.
