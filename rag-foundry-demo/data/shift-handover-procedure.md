# Shift Handover Procedure

## Why handover matters
Most operational surprises happen because information was lost between crews. A
structured handover ensures the incoming shift inherits an accurate picture of hazards,
equipment status, and work in progress. Handover is a controlled activity, not a chat.

## Timing
The outgoing supervisor prepares the handover during the last hour of the shift. The
formal handover meeting occurs at the crew change and runs for no more than 15 minutes.
Both supervisors sign the handover log.

## Handover content
The handover must cover, as a minimum:

- Safety: open hazards, incidents and near-misses this shift, exclusion zones, permits.
- Production: tonnes moved against plan, blocks in progress, grade-control notes.
- Equipment: machines down, defects raised, units on restricted operation.
- People: crew numbers, fatigue concerns, inductions or visitors on site.
- Environment: weather impacts, dust or water management actions, spills.
- Outstanding actions and what the incoming shift is expected to prioritise.

## Equipment status board
The equipment status board is the single source of truth for machine availability. The
outgoing shift updates it before handover. Any machine tagged out must show the reason,
the work order number, and the expected return-to-service time.

## Fatigue and fitness for work
Supervisors confirm each incoming crew member is fit for work. Anyone reporting fatigue,
illness, or the effects of medication must be stood down and reassigned to a safe task.
Fatigue is a reportable hazard and is captured in the handover log.
