# Incident and Near-Miss Reporting Procedure

## Scope
This procedure applies to all incidents, injuries, environmental events, equipment damage,
and near-misses. Reporting is mandatory and **non-punitive** — the goal is to learn and
prevent recurrence, not to assign blame.

## What must be reported
- Any injury or illness, however minor (including first-aid cases).
- **Near-misses**: an event that could have caused harm but did not.
- Dangerous occurrences: uncontrolled energy release, vehicle interaction, fall of ground,
  inrush, or fire.
- Environmental events: hydrocarbon or reagent spills, unauthorised discharge, dust or noise
  exceedances.
- Damage to plant, equipment, or infrastructure.

## How to report a near-miss
1. Make the area safe and notify your supervisor immediately by radio.
2. Within the same shift, log the event in the **SafeTrack** system under "Near-Miss".
3. Record what happened, the location, the time, and the controls that prevented harm.
4. Do not disturb the scene if an investigation may be required.

Near-misses are the most valuable signal we have: report them even if nothing was damaged
and no one was hurt. A reported near-miss today prevents an injury tomorrow.

## Regulator notification
Certain events are **notifiable incidents** under the WHS (Mines) Regulations 2022 (WA) and
must be reported to the regulator (DEMIRS) within the statutory timeframe — for example a
death, serious injury or illness, or a dangerous incident such as an uncontrolled fall of
ground or a mobile plant collision. The **Site Senior Executive** coordinates regulator
notification and preserves the site for any investigation.

## Timeframes
- Verbal notification to supervisor: immediately.
- Written entry in SafeTrack: before the end of shift.
- Notifiable incident to the regulator: within the period prescribed by the Regulations.

## Investigation
The supervisor triages every report and assigns a severity rating. Significant events trigger
an **ICAM** (Incident Cause Analysis Method) investigation to identify root causes and
corrective actions. Corrective actions are tracked to completion in SafeTrack and reviewed at
the weekly safety meeting.
