# Incident and Near-Miss Reporting Procedure

## Scope
This procedure applies to all incidents, injuries, environmental events, equipment
damage, and near-misses on site. Reporting is mandatory and non-punitive — the goal is
to learn and prevent recurrence, not to assign blame.

## What must be reported
- Any injury or illness, however minor (including first-aid cases).
- Near-misses: an event that could have caused harm but did not.
- Dangerous occurrences: uncontrolled energy release, vehicle interaction, fall of ground.
- Environmental events: spills, unauthorised discharge, dust or noise exceedances.
- Damage to plant, equipment, or infrastructure.

## How to report a near-miss
1. Make the area safe and notify your supervisor immediately by radio.
2. Within the same shift, log the event in the SafeTrack system under "Near-Miss".
3. Record what happened, the location, the time, and the controls that prevented harm.
4. Do not disturb the scene if an investigation may be required.

Near-misses are the most valuable signal we have: report them even if nothing was
damaged and no one was hurt. A reported near-miss today prevents an injury tomorrow.

## Timeframes
- Verbal notification to supervisor: immediately.
- Written entry in SafeTrack: before the end of the shift.
- Serious incidents (potential Class 1): the site must notify the regulator within the
  statutory period; the Site Senior Executive coordinates this.

## Investigation
The supervisor triages every report and assigns a severity rating. Significant events
trigger an ICAM investigation to identify root causes and corrective actions. Corrective
actions are tracked to completion in SafeTrack and reviewed at the weekly safety meeting.
