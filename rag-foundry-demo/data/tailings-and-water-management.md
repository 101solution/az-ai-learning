# Tailings Storage and Water Management

## Scope
This procedure covers safe operation of the **tailings storage facility (TSF)** and site water
management. A TSF failure is a catastrophic, low-frequency / high-consequence event, managed
under the operation's tailings management plan and aligned with recognised good practice
(e.g. the Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management).

## Roles
A qualified **Engineer of Record** is responsible for TSF design and integrity. The operation
appoints a **Responsible Tailings Facility Engineer** accountable for day-to-day management,
surveillance, and adherence to the operating manual.

## Deposition and freeboard
Tailings are deposited per the deposition plan to maintain beach geometry and keep the supernatant
pond away from the embankment. **Minimum freeboard** (the distance from water level to crest)
is maintained at all times to contain the design storm and prevent overtopping. Pond position
and freeboard are recorded daily.

## Surveillance and monitoring
- **Daily** visual inspections of the embankment for cracking, seepage, slumping, or erosion.
- **Piezometers and inclinometers** monitor phreatic surface and movement; readings are
  trended against Trigger Action Response Plan (TARP) thresholds.
- Decant and spillway systems are kept clear and functional.
- Formal independent reviews are conducted at the required intervals.

## Water management
Site water is managed to separate clean (diversion) water from contact water. Contact water and
process water are captured, stored, and reused where possible. Discharge to the environment is
only permitted within licence limits, with monitoring of quality (pH, turbidity, metals).

## Wet-season and storm readiness
Before the wet season, freeboard, spillways, pumps, and diversion drains are inspected and the
storm storage allowance confirmed. During a cyclone watch/warning, deposition and pumping plans
are adjusted to maximise containment.

## Emergency triggers
Rising phreatic levels, new seepage, loss of freeboard, or embankment deformation are TARP
triggers. A red trigger initiates the **emergency response plan**, including downstream
notification where required.
